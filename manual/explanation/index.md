# Explanation

Explanation pages are for understanding. They do not teach by step-by-step success and they do not
act as a reference dictionary; instead, they answer the *why* questions behind AIVI.

## Read these when you want the bigger picture

| Question | Page |
| --- | --- |
| Why does AIVI exist at all? | [Why AIVI?](/guide/why-aivi) |
| What changes if I come from imperative code? | [If you are new to functional programming](/explanation/functional-programming-bridge) |
| How should I approach real AIVI code? | [Thinking in AIVI](/guide/thinking-in-aivi) |

## What explanation pages should give you

- the mental model behind values, signals, sources, and pipes
- the reasons AIVI chooses explicit types and closed data
- the design logic behind GTK-first native desktop work
- enough conceptual footing that the tutorials and reference pages make sense faster
